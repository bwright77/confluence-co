## The experience

From the summits of mountains to the bottom of canyons, water connects everything in the American West. On this five-day, four-night trip, students take a deep dive into Colorado's watersheds and the increasingly critical role they play in our lives. Students explore riparian ecosystems, evaluate river health, and complete a river-based Action Project to support a healthy watershed. Along the way, they build their confidence outside — practicing low-impact camping skills, developing outdoor skills, and engaging in place-based activities that connect them with the natural world.

## What students do

- **Day 1 — Orientation and place.** Build group identity and connect with the land.
- **Day 2 — Reading the river.** Tour a fish hatchery, run hands-on water-quality testing, learn to read the river while fishing.
- **Day 3 — Stewardship in action.** Complete a service project that gives back to a river ecosystem.
- **Day 4 — Our own stories about water.** Sensory awareness, sit spots, and whitewater rafting.
- **Day 5 — Making meaning.** Reflection, closing circle, return.

## Why it works

The Colorado Watershed Project blends environmental education, service learning, and outdoor experience in a single immersion. Students don't just learn *about* watersheds — they spend a week inside one, with peers and adults who believe they have something to contribute. Confluence Colorado partners with Cottonwood Institute, whose change theory holds that every student has a gift; when they apply that gift to an issue they're passionate about, they change themselves, their communities, and the world.

## Curriculum alignment

Programming reinforces Colorado academic standards including SC10-GR.8-S.2-GLE.1 (human activities and ecosystem resiliency), RWC10-GR.12-S.1-GLE.2 (effective collaborative groups), PE09-GR.7-S.3-GLE (inclusiveness), and PE09-GR.HS-S.1-GLE.3 (applying problem-solving in nontraditional movement settings).
