## What this program does

Green Workforce Pathways creates a dedicated green-workforce development phase within Confluence Colorado's broader Pathways model. The program runs year-round; the current cycle is funded by Denver CASR from April 2026 through March 2027. Up to 14 youth ages 16–24 are employed and compensated for hands-on training in conservation and natural resource work, with up to 1,400 total paid work hours over the program term.

Participants engage in:

- Community art installations reflecting Indigenous and Hispanic heritage
- Urban garden support and neighborhood street and park beautification projects
- Community events celebrating cultural identity and environmental stewardship
- Career and higher-education exposure sessions with natural-resource industry partners

## Why it matters

Green Workforce Pathways connects young adults — historically facing barriers to employment — directly to careers with the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service and other federal, state, and local agencies, as well as private, community, and higher-education partners.

The program strengthens neighborhood-based environmental stewardship while building a skilled, diverse natural-resource workforce rooted in the communities most impacted by pollution and disinvestment. It also supports the reduction of Denver's greenhouse gas emissions by feeding the pipeline of skilled green workers.

## How we report on it

Confluence Colorado submits a bimonthly check-in to CASR using the form they provide, covering deliverable tracking, budget tracking, and any changes to programming. A final report — including outcomes, budget spend-down, receipts, participant testimonials, and lessons learned — is submitted within 15 days of contract end.
