## The work

A team of roughly ten youth environmental associates, led by a site supervisor, joins native tree and shrub planting along the First Creek corridor at Rocky Mountain Arsenal National Wildlife Refuge. The crew either participates in the FedEx Community Service Day tree-planting event in May or completes remaining planting needs in the week that follows.

Beyond the planting itself, participants engage in environmental education on the cultural history of First Creek and the downstream impacts of restoration on surrounding communities — including the parts of Commerce City and northeast Denver that the creek drains toward as it joins the South Platte.

## Why First Creek

First Creek is a small but important tributary of the South Platte that crosses one of the most ecologically remarkable restored landscapes in the United States: the Rocky Mountain Arsenal National Wildlife Refuge, a former chemical weapons manufacturing site now home to bison, raptors, and recovering native prairie. Erosion along the creek corridor degrades water quality and habitat for the refuge and for downstream communities. Native plantings stabilize the banks, filter runoff, and rebuild riparian habitat.

## The funding chain

This work is supported by a $3,500 subaward from Friends of the Front Range Wildlife Refuges (FFRWR). FFRWR is the prime recipient of a National Fish and Wildlife Foundation grant titled *Restoring Creek Habitat to Prevent Erosion and Improve Water Quality*. Confluence Colorado is one of several subrecipients executing portions of the work.
