## The apprenticeship

The Kady Youth Sheep Camp Apprenticeship carries the heartbeat of our ancestors — a path of returning to the lifeways of sheep and goats, our gentle teachers of patience, balance, and care.

Through the work of our hands and hearts — herding, tending the land, gathering herbs, shaping clay, and weaving fiber — the young learn the old songs again, and keep the fire of our Diné lifeways alive.

*— Roy Kady, Kady Youth Sheep Camp*

## What Confluence does

Confluence Colorado provides the camp with grant writing, fundraising, and program development, so that Roy Kady and the camp's youth can spend their attention on the flock, the land, and the loom rather than on paperwork.

We are also the camp's **fiscal sponsor**. Gifts made to the camp through Confluence Colorado are tax-deductible; Confluence retains discretion and control over the funds and grants them in support of the camp's work.

## Why this work

Raising Navajo-Churro sheep and goats on the high desert is conservation, food, and health practiced as one thing. The flock is stewarded alongside the land that feeds it; the wool becomes the weaving; the herbs and clay gathered on the walk are medicine and craft. Young people learn all of it by doing it, beside elders who carry the songs.

The camp sits in Teec Nos Pos, on the Navajo Nation — far from the South Platte, and rooted in the same conviction that guides our work in Denver: that young people become stewards of a place by being given real work in it.
